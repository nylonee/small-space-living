## Why Your Small Desk Needs a Monitor Arm or Laptop Stand

Before we dive into the products, let's talk about why lifting your screen matters — especially in a compact home office.

**Ergonomics first.** When your laptop or monitor sits directly on the desk, you naturally look downward, straining your neck and upper back. The NHS recommends that the top of your screen should be at or just below eye level. A monitor arm or laptop stand achieves this instantly.

**Space reclaimed.** A gas-spring monitor arm lifts your screen off the desk entirely, leaving the surface clear for a keyboard, mouse, notebook, or even a coffee. A laptop stand angles your machine up, letting you use an external keyboard underneath. Either way, you get back a significant amount of usable desk area.

**Flexibility.** Monitor arms let you tilt, swivel, and rotate your screen between portrait and landscape. Laptop stands offer multiple viewing angles. That flexibility is especially valuable in a small space where your seating or desk arrangement might change throughout the day.

## Buying Guide

### Monitor Arm vs Laptop Stand — Which Do You Need?

**Choose a monitor arm if:** You use a separate monitor (not a laptop) as your primary display. A gas-spring arm gives you full range of motion — height, tilt, swivel, and rotation — and clears the entire desk area underneath the screen. The ErGear and HUANUO are both excellent choices.

**Choose a laptop stand if:** Your main computer is a laptop. A stand lifts the screen to a better viewing angle and prevents overheating. The Lamicall is the premium pick; the Amazon Basics is the budget option that still does the job.

### What to Look For

- **VESA compatibility:** If you're buying a monitor arm, check that your monitor has VESA mounting holes (75×75 mm or 100×100 mm). Most modern monitors do, but ultra-budget models sometimes don't.
- **Weight capacity:** Make sure the arm can handle your monitor's weight. Weigh your screen if you're unsure — a 24-inch monitor typically weighs 3–5 kg.
- **Desk thickness:** Gas-spring arms use clamps that work on desks up to about 8 cm thick. If your desk is thicker (some IKEA worktops are 6–8 cm), check the clamp specifications.
- **Portability:** If you move between home and university or change rooms often, a foldable laptop stand like the Amazon Basics is easier to take with you than a bolted-down monitor arm.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Mounting an arm on a flimsy desk.** Gas-spring arms apply leverage. A cheap hollow-core desk can flex or even crack. If your desk feels lightweight, use the grommet mount option (drills through a small hole) instead of the clamp.
- **Buying a laptop stand that's too small.** Double-check your laptop's diagonal size. The Amazon Basics only fits up to 15.6 inches — if you have a 17-inch work laptop, go for the Lamicall.
- **Forgetting cable management.** A monitor arm frees up desk space, but dangling cables quickly make it look messy again. Both the ErGear and HUANUO have built-in cable routing — use it.
