## Buying Guide

**Size and shape matter.** Rectangular containers use cupboard space far more efficiently than round ones — there's less wasted air between containers and they fit tidily on standard UK kitchen shelves. Measure your cupboard depth before buying to ensure a snug fit, and look for sets that offer a range of sizes rather than a single generic tub.

**Stackability isn't just about stacking.** Look for containers with recessed bases that lock into the lid of the one below (like Sistema's Klip It design). This prevents that avalanche moment when you open the cupboard door and reach for one container only to have three crash down around it.

**Material choice affects longevity.** Clear BPA-free plastic lets you see contents at a glance — essential for busy mornings. Food-grade silicone is best for collapsible designs but can stain over time with strongly coloured foods like turmeric or tomato. Glass is heavier and takes up more space but lasts longer and doesn't stain; for very small kitchens, plastic and silicone remain the smarter choice.

**Lid storage is the real test.** Every set on the market claims to "save space" — the good ones store lids as part of the stack or attached to the container. If the set requires you to store lids separately, you will absolutely lose them. Clip-on or click-in lids that stay with their container are a must for sanity.

**Dishwasher compatibility.** If a container says "hand wash only", cross it off your list immediately. In a small flat with limited counter space, you need everything to go in the dishwasher to keep the kitchen functional.

## FAQ

**Can I microwave Sistema containers safely?**
Yes — Sistema's Klip It range is fully microwave-safe. The collapsible silicone containers in this guide are also microwave-safe. Always remove the lid or crack the seal before microwaving to release steam, and follow the recommended microwave times printed on the base.

**How many containers do I actually need for a small flat?**
For a single person in a studio, a 5-pack of assorted sizes (covering small to large), one collapsible colander, and one set of measuring cups handles every cooking scenario. For two people, add a second 5-pack plus a few larger rectangular units for batch cooks and bulk storage. Start small and add as your needs become clear.

**Are collapsible silicone containers as durable as rigid plastic?**
Modern food-grade silicone of the quality used by Joseph Joseph and comparable brands is extremely durable and heat-resistant. It won't crack or shatter like rigid plastic can, and it performs better in temperature extremes. The trade-off is that silicone can be pierced by sharp utensils and may absorb strong odours — but regular dishwasher cleaning mitigates the latter.

**Can I freeze food in these containers?**
Yes — all the plastic and silicone containers listed here are freezer-safe. Leave 1–2 cm of headspace when freezing liquids to allow for expansion, and always cool cooked food to room temperature before placing it in the freezer. Glass variants are also freezer-safe but take up more room.

**What about glass stackable containers — are they worth it for small kitchens?**
Glass is heavier, doesn't compress or collapse, and takes up more cupboard space than plastic or silicone equivalents. For very small kitchens where every centimetre counts, plastic and silicone are the smarter choice. If you prefer glass for reheating, look for rectangular borosilicate sets with clip-lock lids — they stack well, but be prepared to sacrifice the collapsibility advantage.

**How do I prevent containers from staining?**
Turmeric, tomato-based sauces, and curry can stain plastic and silicone over time. A paste of bicarbonate of soda and water, left to sit for 30 minutes, is remarkably effective at lifting stains. Alternatively, smaller containers can be soaked in diluted bleach for an hour and then thoroughly washed. Dishwasher use helps prevent cumulative staining.
