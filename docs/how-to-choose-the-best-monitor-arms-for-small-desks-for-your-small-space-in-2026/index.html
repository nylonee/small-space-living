What to Look For: Key Features and Specifications

Size Compatibility

First and foremost, ensure the monitor arm is compatible with the size of your monitor. Most monitors today range from 13 inches to over 34 inches in diagonal size. Monitor arms typically have a VESA mount compatibility list or specify the maximum and minimum screen sizes they can support.

Adjustability

Look for monitor arms that offer multiple adjustments: height, tilt, swivel, and pivot. These features allow you to position your monitor exactly where it feels most comfortable for your eyes and hands. In 2026, most high-quality monitor arms will have these capabilities, but always check the specifications.

Stability

Stability is critical to prevent monitors from slipping or tipping over. Look for monitor arms with a gas spring mechanism that provides smooth and controlled movement. The arm should also be sturdy enough to support your monitor without bending or flexing too much under load. A good rule of thumb is to look for arms rated to handle at least 9kg (20 pounds) of weight.

Ease of Installation

Ease of installation can make a big difference in the overall user experience. Monitor arms that require tools and involve multiple steps can be frustrating, especially if you're working alone or don’t have much time. Look for monitor arms that come with clear instructions and minimal tools required.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overlooking the Arm’s Capacity

Ensure that the monitor arm can support your monitor's weight without flexing or tipping. A 13-inch monitor may weigh less than a 27-inch monitor, so make sure you choose an arm with sufficient capacity for your screen size and any accessories (like webcams or microphones) attached to it.

Ignoring Installation Requirements

Some monitor arms come with complex installation instructions that can be challenging. Choose models that are easy to set up, especially if you plan on setting them up alone. Look for those that provide clear, step-by-step instructions and minimal tools required.

Not Considering the Arm’s Range of Motion

A monitor arm that doesn’t allow enough movement can hinder your comfort and productivity. Consider the range of motion needed for your work—will a simple tilt and swivel be sufficient, or do you need more extensive adjustments like full rotation?
