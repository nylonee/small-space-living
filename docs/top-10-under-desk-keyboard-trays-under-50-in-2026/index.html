The Problem with Small Spaces

This is where under-desk keyboard trays shine by offering a compact solution that keeps your desktop clear and clutter-free.

What Are Under Desk Keyboard Trays?

Under desk keyboard trays are specialized compartments designed to slide beneath the edge of your desk. They provide an extra surface area for your keyboard, allowing you to extend your working space without occupying too much of your visible workspace. This is particularly beneficial if you have limited desk space or want a clean look.

Why Choose Under Desk Keyboard Trays?

Space Efficiency

Under desk keyboard trays are perfect for those who don't have much room on their desks. They allow you to use the full length of your work surface while keeping the keyboard hidden until needed, creating a more organized workspace.

Ergonomic Benefits

These trays often come with features that promote better ergonomics. Many models offer adjustable height and tilt settings, which can help reduce strain and improve posture during long hours of typing.

Aesthetically Pleasing

Compared to desktop keyboards, under desk keyboards can make your workspace look tidier by reducing the bulk on top of your desk.

How to Choose the Right Under Desk Keyboard Tray

When selecting an under desk keyboard tray, consider the following factors:

Space Requirements

Measure your desk to ensure you have enough space for the tray and any additional equipment like monitors. The tray should fit comfortably without obstructing your view of the screen.

Ergonomic Features

Look for trays with adjustable height and tilt options. These features can significantly impact your comfort and posture during long work sessions.

Cable Management

If cable management is important, choose a tray that has designated spaces or slots to keep cords organized.

Durability

Check reviews and product descriptions to ensure the material quality of the tray. You want something sturdy that will last through daily use.

Tips for Using Under Desk Keyboard Trays

  1. Adjust Height and Tilt: Take some time to adjust the height and tilt settings until you find a comfortable position.
  2. Cable Management: Use cable ties or clips to keep cables neatly organized, preventing tangling and distractions.
  3. Regular Cleaning: Keep your under desk keyboard tray clean by wiping it down regularly with a microfiber cloth.
