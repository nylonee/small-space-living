What to Look For

1. Size

One of the first things to consider when choosing a standing desk converter is its size. Converters are designed to fit on existing desks or work surfaces without taking up too much space. Ensure that the product you choose has enough clearance for your keyboard and monitor, but doesn’t overcrowd your workspace.

2. Features

Key features to look for include:

  • Height Adjustment: Most converters will have some form of height adjustment, allowing you to switch between sitting and standing positions.
  • Monitor Support: Check if the converter can support multiple monitors or a larger keyboard tray.
  • Stability: A sturdy base ensures that your converter won’t wobble or tip over during use.
  • Storage Options: Some converters offer additional storage space for books, papers, or cable management.

3. Price Range

For this guide, we’ll focus on standing desk converters under £200. This price range offers a good balance between quality and affordability, providing features suitable for most small spaces without breaking the bank.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Overlooking Stability

Always ensure the converter is stable enough to support your weight and any additional equipment you might use. A wobbly desk can be dangerous and may even cause injuries if it tips over.

2. Ignoring Ergonomics

While functionality is important, ergonomics should not be compromised. Look for converters with adjustable height settings that allow you to find the perfect standing position without causing strain or discomfort.

3. Not Considering Monitor Support

If you plan on using multiple monitors, make sure the converter has sufficient space and support for them. Insufficient monitor support can lead to poor posture and decreased productivity.
