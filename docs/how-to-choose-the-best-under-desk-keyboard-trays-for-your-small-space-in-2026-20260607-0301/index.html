Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Overlooking Ergonomics

Ensure the keyboard tray is ergonomically designed to support your wrists and forearms. Poor positioning can lead to discomfort and strain.

2. Ignoring Space Constraints

Measure your desk area before purchasing a keyboard tray. Ensure it will fit comfortably under your desk without obstructing other components or making the workspace too cluttered.

3. Choosing an Overpriced Option

While premium options offer additional features, they may not be necessary for all users. Choose based on your specific needs and budget constraints.

4. Neglecting Compatibility

Ensure the tray is compatible with your desk type and dimensions. Some trays may have limitations that could affect their usefulness in certain setups.

A Simple Decision Framework

  1. Identify Your Needs:
    - Determine if you need a basic or premium model.
    - Consider how many monitors or keyboards you will use.
    - Evaluate the size of your desk and available space under it.

  2. Set a Budget:
    - Decide on a price range that fits your budget (e.g., £25-£100).

  3. Research Options:
    - Compare different models based on features, compatibility, and reviews.
    - Look for product specifications that meet your needs.

  4. Test Before Purchasing:
    - If possible, visit a store or ask for advice from online forums to test the trays in person.
    - Consider purchasing a trial period if available to ensure it works well with your setup.

  5. Evaluate Ergonomics:
    - Ensure the tray supports a natural wrist and arm position.
    - Check for features like height adjustability and depth suitability.
