Why Wall Mounted Shelves Are Essential for Small Spaces

Wall mounted shelves — also called floating shelves — are a staple of small space living for good reason. Unlike bulky bookcases or standalone shelving units, wall mounted shelves use dead wall space that would otherwise go unused. They keep your floors clear, making rooms feel larger and easier to clean.

For UK flats and studios where storage space is often limited, a couple of well-placed floating shelves can make the difference between a chaotic home and a calm, organised one. You can mount them above a desk for paperwork, in the kitchen for spices and jars, in the bathroom for toiletries, or in the living room for display items.

How to Choose the Right Wall Mounted Shelves for Your Small Apartment

1. Weight Capacity

This is the most important factor. A shelf that claims to hold 5 kg and one that holds 15 kg are different products for different purposes. If you are storing ceramic plates, heavy books, or multiple glass bottles, go for a higher weight capacity. For lightweight decor, photo frames, or air plants, a lower capacity is fine.

2. Size and Layout

Measure your wall space before buying. A 45 cm shelf is great for a narrow wall above a desk, while a longer span might suit a living room feature wall. Consider mounting two shelves vertically offset — this creates visual interest and gives you more storage without taking up extra wall width.

3. Finish and Style

Your wall mounted shelves should complement your existing furniture. Black and white finishes are safe bets that work with almost any decor. Wood finishes like oak or walnut add warmth and work well in living rooms and bedrooms. Match the finish to your other furniture for a cohesive look.

4. Installation Considerations

In UK homes, you will typically be mounting into brick, plasterboard, or stud walls. Most shelves come with appropriate fixings for solid walls, but for plasterboard you may need specialist wall plugs. If you are renting, check your tenancy agreement — some landlords allow small shelves, others do not. Always use a spirit level to ensure your shelves are straight.

5. Open versus Closed Storage

Wall mounted shelves are open storage, which means everything on them is visible. This is great for displaying beautiful items, but it also means clutter is on show. Be intentional about what you put on your shelves — use decorative baskets or boxes to corral smaller items and keep the look tidy.

FAQ

Q: Can I install wall mounted shelves in a rented flat?
A: It depends on your tenancy agreement. Most landlords will allow small shelves as long as you fill and paint the holes when you move out. If you are unsure, ask your landlord first. Stick-on adhesive shelves are an alternative if drilling is not permitted.

Q: What tools do I need to install floating shelves?
A: You will need a drill with appropriate masonry or wood drill bits, a screwdriver, a spirit level, a pencil, and a tape measure. Most floating shelves come with the necessary screws and wall plugs.

Q: How much weight can a typical floating shelf hold?
A: It varies by product. Budget shelves typically hold between 5 and 10 kg, while sturdier options like the Heimlove shelves can hold up to 15 kg. Always check the product specifications before loading heavy items.

Q: Are wall mounted shelves suitable for kitchens?
A: Yes, they are great for small kitchens. Use them to store spices, oils, mugs, or cookbooks. Just avoid placing them directly above the hob where heat and grease can be an issue.

Q: Should I buy a set of two or mix and match sizes?
A: Buying a set of two matching shelves is the most cost-effective approach and ensures a consistent look. However, mixing different sizes or finishes can create an attractive gallery wall effect if done thoughtfully.

Q: Do wall mounted shelves damage walls?
A: Any drilling will leave small holes that need filling when you move out. With proper installation, the shelves themselves should not damage the wall surface. Use the correct fixings for your wall type to avoid pulling out.

Q: What is the difference between floating shelves and bracket shelves?
A: Floating shelves have concealed brackets that slide into the shelf body, creating a seamless look. Bracket shelves have visible L-shaped or decorative supports beneath. Floating shelves tend to look cleaner and more modern, which is why they are preferred for small apartment storage.
