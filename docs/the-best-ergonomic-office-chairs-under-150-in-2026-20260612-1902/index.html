Buying Guide

When choosing an ergonomic office chair under £150, consider the following factors:

1. Lumbar Support

Look for chairs that provide adequate support to your lower back. This is crucial in preventing long-term discomfort and potential injuries.

2. Seat Height Adjustment

Ensure the chair can be adjusted to fit different desk heights and body types. Adjustable seats are particularly useful if you work from standing desks or need frequent height changes.

3. Armrests

Armrests should provide support for your arms while working, especially if you spend a lot of time typing or using a mouse. Flip-up armrests can be convenient for moving around and accessing documents on the desk.

4. Swivel Mechanism

A good swivel base allows easy movement in any direction without straining your back or neck. Look for chairs with a five-star base that provides smooth rotation.

5. Fabric Quality

Breathable mesh fabric helps prevent overheating during long work sessions, while high-density foam cushions offer excellent comfort and support.

A2: It’s important to make adjustments as needed to ensure you maintain the correct posture. At minimum, check and adjust your chair daily to accommodate any changes in your body position throughout the day.

Q3: Are these chairs easy to clean?
A3: Many of the mesh-backed chairs are easier to clean than fabric ones due to their breathable nature. However, always refer to the manufacturer’s care instructions for specific guidance.

Q4: How do I know which chair is best for me?
A4: Consider your specific needs and preferences. If you have lower back pain, prioritize chairs with robust lumbar support. For those who frequently move around their workspaces, a chair with easy swivel mechanisms will be beneficial.
