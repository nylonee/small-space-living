How to Choose the Best Monitor Arms for Small Desks in Your Small Space in 2026

When you're dealing with a small workspace, maximizing every inch of your desk can make all the difference. A monitor arm is an essential piece of furniture that can transform how you work and live in a compact space. In this guide, we’ll explore what to look for in a monitor arm, compare top options, highlight common mistakes, and provide a simple decision framework.

What to Look For

Size

When choosing a monitor arm, consider the size of your screen(s). Most monitors used today range from 13 inches to 34 inches diagonally. Monitor arms are typically designed for screens in this size range:

  • ErGear Single Monitor Arm: Suitable for 13-34 inch screens.
  • HUANUO Single Monitor Arm: Works with 13-32 inch screens.

Features

Monitor arms come equipped with various features that can make your workspace more efficient and comfortable. Here are some key features to consider:

  • Adjustability: Look for arms that allow you to adjust the height, tilt, swivel, and rotate the screen.
  • Weight Capacity: Ensure the arm can support the weight of your monitor(s). Common capacities range from 3 kg to 12 kg (6.5 lbs - 26.4 lbs).
  • Mounting Options: Some arms use a C-clamp or grommet mount for attaching to desks, while others might have VESA mounts.
  • Gas Spring: A gas spring mechanism provides smooth and effortless adjustment of the screen.

Price Range

Monitor arms can vary significantly in price depending on their features. Here are some examples within different price ranges:

  • ErGear Single Monitor Arm (around £28.48): Offers a good balance between flexibility and affordability.
  • HUANUO Single Monitor Arm (around £25.99): A budget-friendly option that still provides essential functionality.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overlooking the Size

Choosing an arm that’s too small for your screen can limit your flexibility. Conversely, a larger monitor might not fit on a smaller arm. Always measure both your screen and desk before purchasing.

Ignoring Ergonomics

A well-designed monitor arm should help reduce strain by allowing you to position your screen at the optimal height and angle. Poorly designed arms may lead to uncomfortable neck or eye strain.

Not Considering Future Needs

If you plan on using multiple monitors, look for an arm that can accommodate them. Additionally, consider whether you might need more features like cable management in the future.

Simple Decision Framework

  1. Determine Your Screen Size and Quantity: Know what screens you'll be working with.
  2. Evaluate Your Desk Space: Consider the available desk area to ensure the arm fits comfortably.
  3. Prioritize Features:
    - Height Adjustment: Essential for ergonomic comfort.
    - Tilt and Swivel: Useful for reducing eye strain and neck fatigue.
    - Weight Capacity: Make sure it can support your monitor(s).
  4. Budget Considerations: Set a budget range and prioritize features based on price.

Conclusion

Choosing the right monitor arm is crucial for optimizing your small workspace in 2026. Remember, investing in a good monitor arm can pay off by improving your work environment and reducing physical strain over time. Make an informed decision that fits your specific needs and enjoy the benefits of a better workspace!
