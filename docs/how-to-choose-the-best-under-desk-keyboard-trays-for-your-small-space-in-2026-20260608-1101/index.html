When it comes to small spaces, every inch of usable desk real estate counts. One overlooked yet crucial element is an under desk keyboard tray. These trays not only save space but also enhance ergonomics and comfort during long hours at your desk. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore what to look for in a good under desk keyboard tray, compare top 5 options, highlight common mistakes to avoid, and provide you with a simple decision framework.

What to Look For

Size

The size of the under desk keyboard tray is crucial as it needs to fit comfortably beneath your existing desk. A general rule is that for an ergonomic setup, the depth should be about half the width of your monitor or laptop. For example, if you have a 24-inch monitor, your keyboard tray should ideally be around 12 inches deep.

Features

Look for specific features that can help optimize your workspace:

  • Adjustability: Height and tilt adjustment are key to maintaining good posture.
  • Stability: A sturdy construction prevents the tray from wobbling or tipping over.
  • Material Quality: Durable materials like metal or high-density plastic ensure longevity.
  • Compatibility: Check if it fits under your desk model without causing damage.

Price Range

For a UK audience, you can find quality under desk keyboard trays in the range of £20 to £150. While more expensive models often offer premium features, many mid-range options still deliver excellent value for money.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Choosing an Overly Large Tray: Ensure the tray is not wider than half the monitor or laptop's width; otherwise, it can look messy and take up too much space.
  2. Neglecting Ergonomics: Prioritize trays with good height and angle adjustments for a more comfortable working posture.
  3. Ignoring Stability: Avoid trays that lack robust construction or non-slip feet, as they may wobble during use.
  4. Overlooking Compatibility: Make sure the tray fits well under your desk without causing damage.

Simple Decision Framework

  1. Measure Your Desk Space: Determine the exact dimensions of your workspace to ensure the chosen tray will fit comfortably and not clutter your area.
  2. Consider Ergonomic Needs: Assess whether you need full adjustability or basic height and tilt options based on how you work.
  3. Evaluate Budget Constraints: Decide between budget-friendly options with fewer features versus premium models that offer more comprehensive adjustments.
  4. Check Customer Reviews: Read feedback from other users to gauge the reliability and effectiveness of different trays.
