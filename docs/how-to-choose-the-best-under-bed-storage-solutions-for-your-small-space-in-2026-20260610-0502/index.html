What to Look For in Under Bed Storage Solutions

Size

When it comes to under bed storage containers, size is everything. You want something that can comfortably fit beneath your bed frame without protruding or being too cumbersome. Generally, a low-profile container with dimensions around 31x15.7x6 inches (L x W x H) works well. This ensures you have enough space for all your essentials while maintaining the aesthetic integrity of your room.

Features

Key features to look out for include:

  • Handles: Easy access is crucial, and handles make it simple to lift and move containers.
  • Zippers or Latches: While zippers are convenient, latches can provide better security. Some modern options offer both.
  • Transparent Windows: A window lets you see what’s inside without opening the container, which can be handy when you’re short on space and need to manage multiple items efficiently.

Price Range

Prices vary depending on brand, quality, and additional features. Most under bed storage containers fall within the £20-£50 range for basic models, with premium options pushing into the £100+ bracket. For this article, we’ll focus on options that cost around £29.99 or less.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Overlooking the Size: Make sure you measure the underbed space before purchasing containers. Containers that are too big or too small can clutter up your room.
  2. Neglecting Security: While functionality is important, don’t compromise on security. Look for containers with sturdy locks if you need to store valuable items.
  3. Skipping Transparency: A clear window can be a lifesaver when you’re trying to organize multiple items without having to dig around.
  4. Ignoring Design and Aesthetics: Under bed storage doesn’t have to look drab. Choose containers that blend well with your existing decor or add a touch of style.

Decision Framework

  1. Assess Your Needs: What do you need to store? Clothes, shoes, accessories, or other items?
  2. Measure the Space: Use a tape measure to ensure the containers will fit comfortably under your bed.
  3. Consider Functionality and Features: Think about what features are important to you (handles, zippers, windows).
  4. Budget Constraints: Set a budget range and stick to it. Stick within £50 for basic options or up to £100 for premium containers.
