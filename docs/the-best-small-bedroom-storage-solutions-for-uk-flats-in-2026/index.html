Buying Guide

When choosing small bedroom storage, start by measuring your available space. For under bed containers, measure the gap between the floor and your bed frame — you need at least 15 centimetres of clearance for most storage bins. Bedroom storage boxes with wheels are easier to slide out but may require more clearance than flat-bottomed designs.

For vacuum bags, consider whether you own a vacuum cleaner with a standard hose fitting. If you don't, choose a set that includes a hand pump. Jumbo bags (around 100 by 80 centimetres) fit king-size duvets, while large bags (80 by 60 centimetres) handle single and double bedding. Keep bags in their original packaging between seasons so you don't lose the sealing clips.

For hanging closet organisers, check that your wardrobe rail is securely fixed to the wall. A rail that's only held by plastic fittings may not support the weight of a fully loaded organiser. Measure your wardrobe's hanging height too — you need at least 80 centimetres of vertical space for a standard 5-shelf organiser to hang without bunching at the bottom.

FAQ

Q: Will under bed storage containers work with a divan bed?
A: It depends on the bed. Some divan beds have drawers built in, but others have a solid base with no floor clearance. Check your bed's specifications before buying. For standard divans, look for slimline containers that are 15 centimetres or less in height.

Q: Can vacuum storage bags damage my clothes or bedding?
A: Not when used correctly. Avoid compressing delicate fabrics like silk or wool for extended periods — the pressure can set creases. For cotton bedding, duvets, and synthetic fabrics, vacuum storage is perfectly safe and won't cause damage.

Q: How do I stop my hanging closet organiser from sagging?
A: Distribute weight evenly across all shelves rather than loading the top shelf only. Also ensure the hook or clip is properly seated on the wardrobe rail — some organisers come with anti-slip covers that help keep them in place.

Q: What's the best way to organise a small wardrobe without building anything?
A: Hanging organisers are your best bet. Combine one for folded clothes with over-door hooks on the back of the wardrobe door for accessories. Use the top shelf for vacuum bags and rarely-used items, and keep everyday clothes in the hanging organiser for easy access.

Q: Are these storage solutions suitable for student accommodation?
A: Absolutely. Every product recommended here can be installed without tools and removed without leaving marks or damage. Vacuum bags are especially popular with students because they let you pack an entire term's bedding into a single suitcase.
