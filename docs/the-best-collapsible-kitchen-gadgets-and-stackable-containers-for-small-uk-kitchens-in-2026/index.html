Why Collapsible and Stackable Kitchen Gear Works for Small Spaces

Before we get into specific products, let's talk about the two key strategies for maximising kitchen storage.

Collapsible gadgets are designed to fold or flatten when not in use. Think measuring cups that go from full-size to paper-thin, or colanders that compress into a flat disc. They're ideal for items you use regularly but don't need to keep permanently on display.

Stackable containers use modular, uniform shapes that allow you to build upwards rather than outwards. Instead of having mismatched tubs that waste air space in your cupboards, stackable sets fit together like building blocks, making the most of every centimetre of vertical storage.

Used together, these two approaches can double or even triple the usable space in your kitchen cupboards — without costing a fortune.

Buying Guide: What to Look For

Material Quality

The best collapsible kitchen gadgets use food-grade silicone that won't degrade or absorb odours. For stackable containers, look for BPA-free plastic or borosilicate glass. Sistema products are all BPA-free, and Joseph Joseph uses high-quality silicone throughout.

Stackability

Not all "stackable" containers are created equal. Look for rectangular or square shapes that pack together without wasted space, and check that different sizes in the range are designed to nest compatibly. Sistema's Klip It range is particularly good at this.

Ease of Cleaning

Anything that touches food needs to be easy to clean. Dishwasher-safe is the gold standard. All the products in this guide are dishwasher-safe, but check the manufacturer's instructions for lid placement (some lids should go on the top rack).

Seal Quality

For food storage containers, a proper leak-proof seal is essential. Look for silicone gaskets and sturdy clip mechanisms. Sistema's Klip It range excels here — the four-side locking system creates a genuinely watertight seal.

Q: Are collapsible kitchen gadgets durable enough for daily use?
A: Absolutely. Joseph Joseph and similar brands use high-quality silicone and reinforced frames that hold up well to regular use. The collapsible colander, for example, has a rigid internal frame that doesn't weaken over time.

Q: Can I put collapsible silicone containers in the microwave?
A: Yes, but always vent the lid first. All the silicone products in this guide are microwave-safe, but remove any metal clips or clasps before reheating.

Q: How much space can I actually save with collapsible gadgets?

Q: What's the best way to organise stackable containers?
A: Remove lids and store them separately, then stack the bases by size. Sistema containers have lids that clip together, so you can keep matching lids with their bases or store them as a separate stack. Label the bases so you can grab the right one without rummaging.
