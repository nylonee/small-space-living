What to Look for When Choosing an Ergonomic Office Chair

1. Size and Frame

Firstly, consider the size of your space and body dimensions. An ergonomic office chair should fit comfortably within your work area while ensuring you can move freely around it. The frame needs to be sturdy enough to support your weight without feeling too heavy or bulky in a small room.

2. Seat Size and Depth

The seat should be large enough to accommodate your thighs comfortably, with the minimum depth being about 45cm (17.7 inches). Ensure that there’s adequate space for your legs to rest on the floor when sitting fully back in the chair.

3. Back Support

Look for a chair with adjustable back support as it can help alleviate lower back pain by providing proper lumbar support. Ideally, the backrest should be high enough to provide adequate support up to the shoulder blades and adjustable from side-to-side or forward/backward.

4. Armrests and Height Adjustability

Adjustable armrests are crucial for supporting your arms and shoulders. They should be at a comfortable height to rest your hands on when typing, and they can reduce strain on your neck and shoulders. The chair’s height adjustability ensures that you can sit in the correct posture with your feet flat on the ground.

5. Wheel Quality

Given the small spaces involved, smooth-rolling wheels are essential for easy mobility. Look for chairs equipped with quality casters that don’t make too much noise or get stuck. Four-wheel designs provide better stability and comfort than two-wheel options.

6. Price Range

For this guide, we’re focusing on office chairs under £150. This range offers a good balance between functionality and affordability for those looking to furnish their small workspace without breaking the bank.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Neglecting Back Support

A lack of proper back support can lead to significant health issues such as lower back pain and poor posture over time. Ensure that the chair you choose offers adjustable lumbar support to maintain a neutral spine position while working.

2. Ignoring Armrests

Armrests should be properly adjusted to support your arms and reduce strain on your shoulders and neck. Poorly positioned armrests can lead to discomfort and even injuries over extended use.

3. Choosing Too Small a Chair

An office chair that is too small for you will not provide adequate comfort or support, leading to physical discomfort during long work sessions. Measure your space carefully to ensure the chair fits comfortably within it.

4. Overlooking Wheel Quality

Cheap casters can make noise and get stuck, disrupting your workflow and potentially damaging floors. Invest in quality casters that are quiet and durable for a smoother working experience.

A Simple Decision Framework

Step 1: Assess Your Needs

  • Are you primarily concerned with back support or armrest comfort?
  • How often do you plan to use the chair? Daily work sessions or occasional use?

Step 2: Measure Your Space

  • Determine if the chair needs to fit a specific space, such as under a desk or next to a window.
  • Ensure there’s enough clearance for easy movement around the chair.

Step 3: Set a Budget

  • Stick to your budget constraints while prioritizing quality and features that meet your needs.

Step 4: Research Online Reviews

  • Look for reviews from other users who have similar workspaces and needs as yours.
  • Pay attention to comments about comfort, durability, and ease of use.

Step 5: Test Before Purchase (If Possible)

  • If you can visit the store or have a chance to test the chair in person, do so. This will give you an idea of how it feels when you sit for extended periods.
  • Observe if adjustments are smooth and easy to make without assistance.
