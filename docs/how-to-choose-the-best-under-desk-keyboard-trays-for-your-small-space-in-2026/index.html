How to Choose the Best Under Desk Keyboard Trays for Your Small Space in 2026

When it comes to optimizing your small workspace, an under desk keyboard tray can make a significant difference by freeing up precious tabletop space and improving ergonomics. This guide will help you choose the perfect keyboard tray that fits your needs while maximizing efficiency in your compact living or working environment.

What to Look for in an Under Desk Keyboard Tray

Size

The first thing to consider is the size of your workspace and any existing furniture. Ensure that the keyboard tray dimensions fit seamlessly under your desk without obstructing other items or creating a cluttered look. For most home offices, a 24-inch by 12-inch tray should suffice, but those with larger desks might benefit from a 30-inch by 15-inch option.

Features

Adjustable Height and Angle

Adjustability is key for ergonomic comfort. Look for trays that can be adjusted in height to support various typing positions—sitting or standing—and angle options to prevent wrist strain.

Stability

A stable tray ensures that your keyboard doesn’t shift around, especially if you’re moving a mouse or other accessories. Anti-tip features and non-slip pads are great for added security.

Compatibility

Ensure the tray is compatible with your existing keyboard size and shape. Some trays come in fixed sizes while others offer adjustable inserts to accommodate different keyboards.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Not Considering Ergonomics

Investing in a good keyboard tray means prioritizing ergonomics for your wrists and back. Ensure that the tray supports neutral wrist positioning and offers adjustable height options.

2. Overlooking Stability

A poorly designed tray can lead to spills or shift during use, causing distractions and potential damage to your workspace. Opt for trays with anti-tip features and non-slip pads.

3. Ignoring Compatibility Issues

Make sure the tray fits seamlessly with your current setup without requiring additional adapters or inserts that might add bulk or reduce functionality.

Simple Decision Framework

  1. Measure Your Workspace: Determine the available space under your desk to ensure a good fit.
  2. Consider Ergonomic Needs: Prioritize trays with adjustable height and angle options for better comfort.
  3. Check Stability Features: Ensure the tray is secure on your desk, especially if you work standing up or frequently move items.
  4. Review Compatibility: Confirm that the tray will fit your keyboard without requiring additional tools or adapters.
