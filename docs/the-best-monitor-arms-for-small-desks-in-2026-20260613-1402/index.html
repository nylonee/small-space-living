Buying Guide

Key Features to Look For

  1. Compatibility: Ensure the monitor arm is compatible with your specific screen and VESA mount.
  2. Adjustability: Check if you can adjust the height, tilt, swivel, and rotation.
  3. Load Capacity: Consider how heavy your monitor will be and ensure the arm can support it without compromising stability.
  4. Ergonomics: Choose an arm that allows for a natural and comfortable viewing angle to reduce eye strain.
  5. Installation Ease: Look for arms that come with clear instructions or are easy to set up tool-free.

Best Practices

  • Space Management: Opt for arms that can fold down or have a compact design when not in use to save desk space.
  • User Reviews: Check online reviews and ratings from other users who have purchased the same product.
  • Warranty and Support: Ensure you choose an arm with good customer support and warranty options.

Q: Can I get a monitor arm under £50?

Q: Which brand offers the best long-term reliability?

Q: How do I choose between a gas spring or ball bearing arm?

A: Gas springs offer smooth and consistent movement but might have limited adjustment options. Ball-bearing arms provide more flexibility but can be noisier and may require regular maintenance.
